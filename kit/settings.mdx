When you first create your Kit account, it's important that you configure it for success. The good news is that it's simple to do.

In this document, you'll discover what each setting is for and choice recommendations where appropriate.

## Settings

<Card title="Settings" icon="shield-check" horizontal>
  Configurable options for your Kit account.
</Card>

Once logged in to your Kit account, navigate to the menu option entitled '**Settings**', which can be found at the bottom-left corner of the desktop.

#### Multi-factor authentication

This adds a layer of additional security to your Kit account, in case your login credentials are stolen. When switched on, you'll be challenged for a unique one-time code to enter to access your account.

<Frame>
  ![Image](/images/image-8.png)
</Frame>

There are two options you can switch on; Authenticator app and SMS message.

#### Authenticator app

When you enable this option, you'll be prompted to enter a one-time passcode which is generated by your preferred authenticator app. If you don't already have one, Google's **Authenticator** **app** is a good option, and it's free to use.

<Frame>
  ![Image](/images/image-9.png)
</Frame>

You'll find the app available in both the Apple Store and Google Play. To set it up, scan the QR code presented to you when you toggle the Authenticator app in your Kit account.

<Frame>
  ![Image](/images/image-10.png)
</Frame>

Once you successfully submit your unique six-digit number provided by your authenticator, you'll see this message pop up to confirm that you have enabled multi-factor authentication.

<Frame>
  ![Image](/images/image-11.png)
</Frame>

**SMS Authentication**

If you prefer to use SMS authentication, or add it to give more convenience by having two ways in which to authenticate your account, you can switch that option on too.

<Frame>
  ![Image](/images/image-12.png)
</Frame>

You'll be asked for the country code for your mobile phone and your phone number. When challenged, you need to enter the unique six-digit number sent to your mobile.

<Note>
  It's worth noting that SMS messaging is not as reliable as a messaging service if you are located in the United Arab Emirates (UAE) region. An authenticator app would be a more reliable option in this scenario.
</Note>

### Notifications

<Card title="Notifications" icon="shield-check" horizontal>
  Select which notifications, if any, you wish to receive from Kit.
</Card>

When you click on the '**Notifications**' menu option, you'll see a list of activities for which you can configure your notifications preferences.

<Frame>
  ![Image](/images/image-13.png)
</Frame>

The **Account Summary** can be configured to be sent either weekly or monthly. Other notifications are normally sent hourly so as not to overwhelm your inbox if you have a busy account.

Available notifications are determined by which Kit features you have configured for use; e.g. if you're not selling via Kit's Commerce platform, you won't see 'Purchases' as an option.

If you decide you don't want to receive any notifications from Kit, there is a button that will turn them all off with a single click.

<Tip>
  The Notifications centre auto-saves your preferences, so by checking or unchecking an option, kit will save it for you without having to click any other button.
</Tip>

#### Business Name

This can be whatever you want it to be, but should be reflective of your business or brand and should make sense to your subscribers when they see references to it.

You can change the name value if you decide to rebrand at any point. Remember to hit the '**Save** **changes**' button once you've made any changes.

---

#### Saved Colours

You can save up to ten (10) brand colours so that they are conveniently accessible whilst you're creating any assets in your Kit account, including emails, Products, landing pages, forms, etc.

To save a colour, click on any of the empty swatches and select a colour from the colour picker, or type in the hex code value, if you know it.

If you want to delete a colour, simply click the swatch and hit the trash can icon. Deleting a colour from your palette does not impact any existing asset.

<Frame>
  ![Image](/images/image-15.png)
</Frame>

### Email

<Card title="Email" icon="shield-check" horizontal>
  Manage your 'from' email addresses, sending domains, and default timezone.
</Card>

In order to send emails from your Kit account, you need to have at least one 'from' email address, and it needs to be **confirmed** before you start sending.

<Frame>
  ![Xnapper 2026 07 02 17 29 21](/images/Xnapper-2026-07-02-17.29.21.png)
</Frame>

You can have multiple email addresses from which to send your emails, and one must be marked as the 'Default' address.  You can switch your default 'from' email address if you wish, provided it's with another confirmed email address.

To add another email address, click the 'Add from address' link and enter the details. Once added, click the 'Add Email' button and

<Frame>
  ![Image](/images/image-4.png)
</Frame>

After adding the new email address, Kit will send you an email containing a link that you must click to confirm that you have access to the email address. Until you click that link, your email address will indicate that it's 'pending', and therefore unable to be used.

<Frame>
  ![Xnapper 2026 07 02 17 35 28](/images/Xnapper-2026-07-02-17.35.28.png)
</Frame>

The email you receive from Kit will look like the image below; you must click the button to confirm your email address.

<Frame>
